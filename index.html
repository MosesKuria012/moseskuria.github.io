THE ORIGIN

My journey in technology started with video games. Having not grown up with technology up until I was 15 resulted in the obsession of playing games with my mom's new touch screen phone in the evenings because it also dubled as a her work phone during the day. The only rule was to ensure it was fully charged by morning 😂. The curiosity to learn how games are made and why they work on certain phones and not others led me into a rabbit hole that exposed me to software development.

It was during my 1st year of campus that I had a programming unit that exposed me to computer programming. The exposure to C and C++opened a pandora's box in my mind. Writing code never felt natural.

Then came networking. Pursuing CCNA 200-301 and exposure to networking architecture of how systems communicate clicked in a way nothing else had. I graduated with a BSc. in Electrical & Electronics Engineering from Kenyatta University and cloud was the natural next frontier.

Today I specialize in AWS architecture, Terraform automation, and production reliability. I've cut provisioning time by 85%, built event-driven pipelines that govern themselves, and kept systems at 99.9% uptime for thousands of concurrent users.

I'm now deepening my infrastructure-as-code practice — moving from console-first to Terraform-native, building reusable modules and GitOps pipelines that make cloud environments reproducible by default. Twelve technical write-ups document the work in progress.
